Project Objective

For senior capstone at the University of Maryland, my team partnered with the UMD Sensors and Actuators Laboratory to design and fabricate a 3-DOF aquatic robotic manipulator. The intent of this system was to integrate with the Blue-ROV2 platform, enabling researchers to remotely sample oyster populations in the Chesapeake Bay.

Oyster sampling is a uniquely challenging task. Juvenile oysters naturally cement themselves to their surroundings by secreting a calcium carbonate solution. Traditional methods of oyster harvesting involve using either labor-intensive hand-tonging, or destructively dredging the sea floor. A robotic tool intended to sample wild oysters must therefore be capable of forcefully prying oysters from their substrate.

  • $500 Production Budget
  • 3lb Weight Limit
  • Minimum IP68 Protection Rating
Solution

To meet these requirements, my team developed AquatiClaw an SLA-printed robotic manipulator.

This project ended up winning first place at the 2024 Mechanical Engineering Design Expo!

How Did I Contribute?

My design project involved finding a way to actuate the robotic end effector. Due to the strict force requirements of aquatic sampling, I settled on using a custom-built linear actuator to drive the "claws".
